Depth vs Speed:

Lower depth means slower wave velocity, leading to shorter wavelength ($\lambda_2 < \lambda_1$) and wave bending!

1. Deep Zone

v₁ = 1.60 m/s

λ₁ = 34.0 cm

2. Shallow Zone

v₂ = 0.80 m/s

Daytime Behavior

Ground air is hot, higher air is cool. Sound speeds are faster near ground, causing sound waves to refract upwards away from ground.

Nighttime Behavior

Ground air cools quickly, higher air is warmer (temperature inversion). Sound moves slower near ground, refracting downwards towards the audience.

SNELL'S LAW RELATION

$$\frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_2} = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r}$$

What happens to wave frequency as it crosses from deep to shallow water?

No Change!

🌅

How do sea waves change direction as they approach a shoreline beach?

Bends Towards Shore

Because the shore is shallower, the waves refract, slow down, and bend to become parallel to the beach shape.

🚢

Why are concrete harbor barriers built with narrow water openings?

Energy Dispersion

Narrow gaps cause strong diffraction, spreading the wave energy and creating calm waters inside the port zone.

📻

Why do sound waves diffract much easier around corners than light waves?

Wavelength Match

Sound has a long wavelength similar to door or corner widths. Light has an ultra-short wavelength ($10^{-7}$m).

Math of Sound Interference

In KSSM, we determine the distance between adjacent antinodes (loud lines) $x$ by modeling wave kinematics using parameters $a$, $x$, $D$ and $\lambda$.

a

Speaker separation

x

Distance between loud sounds

D

Distance to observer

λ

Wavelength

$$\lambda = \frac{a \cdot x}{D} \quad \implies \quad x = \frac{\lambda \cdot D}{a}$$
